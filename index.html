Domestic Flights

Australian Capital Cities

The capital cities of Australia serve as a gateway for the rest of the country, with connections possible to almost anywhere in Australia via one of these cities.

Understanding how these cities service domestic passengers is vital as it highlights key destinations based on demand and showcases the current services available.

Flight Paths

The connnections from each capital city indicate the in-demand destinations for passengers. Larger cities have more flight routes though them such as Melbourne, Sydney and Brisbane. Many regional locations are only accessible by flight interstate, especially for larger states such as Queensland, New South Wales and Western Australia.

Top Destinations

The top destinations by average passenger count for each capital city are primilary other capital cities. However, other popular destinations are tourist destinations in Queensland, such as Cairns and the Gold Coast. For Perth, many of the top destinations are interstate.

Total Flights

During the 2010s, it can be seen that there was mininal growth in the number of flights transiting through each capital city. The peak flight periods for each city can be seen through the peaks and dips within each year. For most capital cities, flight numbers dip during February of each year.

During the Covid-19 lockdowns, many flights were grounded, with minor peaks during this period corresponsing to easing lockdown restrictions. However post 2021, flight numbers have not yet returned back to pre-lockdown numbers largely due to the impacts this period had on the airline industry.
